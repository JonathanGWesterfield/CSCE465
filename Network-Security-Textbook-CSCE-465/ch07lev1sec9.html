7.9. Homework Problems

1.

If m and n are any two positive integers, show that m/gcd(m,n) and n/gcd(m,n) are relatively prime. [Hint: use the result of Euclid's algorithm.]

2.

If a and b are relatively prime, and bc is a multiple of a, show that c is a multiple of a. [Hint: use the result of Euclid's algorithm.]

3.

In mod n arithmetic, the quotient of two numbers r and m is a number q such that mq = r mod n. Given r, m, and n, how can you find q? How many qs are there? Under what conditions is q unique? [Hint: mq = r mod n iff there is an integer k such that qm + kn = r. Divide by gcd(m,n).]

4.

In the final step of Euclid's algorithm for finding gcd(m,n), we get u and v such that um + vn = 0. Is |um| (which = |vn|) the least common multiple of m and n?

5.

Prove the general case of the Chinese Remainder Theorem. [Hint: z1 is relatively prime to z2z3...zk.]

6.

Show that each row of the Zn* multiplication table is a rearrangement of the 1 row. [Hint: multiply the row by the inverse of its first element.]

7.

For what type of number n is φ(n) largest (relative to n)?

8.

For what type of number n is φ(n) smallest (relative to n)?

9.

Is it possible for φ(n) to be bigger than n?

10.

If n = p1α1p2α2p3α3...pkαk where pi is prime, what is φ(n)?

11.

In RSA, what is the probability that something to be encrypted will not be in Zn*?

12.

Euler's Theorem Variant states that for all a in Zn*, akφ(n)+1 = a mod n. As stated in §7.8.1 A Generalization of Euler's Theorem, if n is the product of two distinct primes, akφ(n)+1 = a mod n for all a in Zn. For what other forms of n will this be true? For what forms of n does it fail?

13.

Prove that if n = 2α0p1α1p2α2p3α3...pkαk where pi are distinct odd primes and αi > 0 for i > 0, then, mod n, 1 will have 2k square roots if α0 1, 2k+1 square roots if α0 = 2, and 2k+2 square roots if α0 3. [Hints: use the Chinese Remainder Theorem to show that a number is a square root of 1 mod n iff it is a square root of 1 mod each of the prime power factors; show that 1 and -1 are the only square roots of 1 mod a power of an odd prime; finally, find the square roots of 1 mod a power of 2.]
