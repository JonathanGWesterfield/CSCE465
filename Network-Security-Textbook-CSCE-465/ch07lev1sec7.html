7.7. Euler's Totient Function

The symbol φ(n) is known as Euler's totient function and is defined as the number of elements in Zn*. For instance, φ(10) is 4, since Z10* is {1,3,7,9}. You may remember how pervasive the totient function was in Chapter 6 Public Key Algorithms.

Given n, can we calculate φ(n)? Suppose n is prime. What is φ(n)? That's fairly easyÂZn* is {1,2,...,n-1}. So φ(n) is n-1.

What is φ(n) when n = pα, where p is prime and α > 0? Only multiples of p are not relatively prime to pα, and every pth number is a multiple of p, so there are pα-1 of them less than pα. So φ(pα) = pα - pα-1 = (p-1)·pα-1.

What is φ(n) when n = pq and p and q are relatively prime? Here's where we get to use the Chinese Remainder Theorem. The Chinese Remainder Theorem says there is a one-to-one correspondence between numbers m in Zpq and pairs of numbers mp in Zp and mq in Zq for which mp = m mod p and mq = m mod q. We'll show that m is relatively prime to pq iff mp is relatively prime to p and mq is relatively prime to q.

If m in Zpq is relatively prime to pq, then there are integers u and v such that um + vpq = 1. Substituting m = mp + kp, we get ump + (uk + vq)p = 1, so mp is relatively prime to p. Similarly, mq is relatively prime to q.

Conversely, if mp in Zp is relatively prime to p and mq in Zq is relatively prime to q, then there are integers up, vp, uq, vq such that upmp + vpp = 1 and uqmq + vqq = 1. Since mp = m - kp for some k, and mq = m - lq for some l, we have upm + (vp - upk)p = 1 and uqm + (vq - uql)q = 1. Multiplying these together gives (upuqm + up(vq - uql)q + uq(vp - upk))m + (vp - upk)(vq - uql)pq = 1, so m is relatively prime to pq.

So there is a one-to-one correspondence between numbers m in Zpq* and pairs of numbers mp in Zp* and mq in Zq*. So φ(pq) = φ(p)φ(q).
